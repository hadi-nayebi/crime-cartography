# Contributor workflow — working terms v0.1

Status: **proposed for public discussion**. This document describes the system
we intend to test. It is not an employment offer, compensation promise,
contest, or active point-redemption program. Operative terms require a future
effective date, participant consent, and appropriate professional review.

Canonical project terms and statuses live in
[`context/PARTICIPATION.md`](../context/PARTICIPATION.md) and
[`context/GLOSSARY.md`](../context/GLOSSARY.md). This file is a detailed workflow
annex; when its wording conflicts with the shared context, the shared context
wins.

## 1. Project

**Crime Cartography** is a human-managed, agent-operated production project for
sourced crime-related videos. The dedicated Crime Cartography channel is the
only public home for the series. The public repository contains production
machinery and reproducibility records; finished audiovisual works are not
licensed under the repository's MIT software license.

## 2. Harness manager

During launch, **Hadi is the harness manager and editor**. He controls release,
handles consequential judgment, and remains accountable for the channel.
Management may later expand to a small team under published roles, access
controls, conflict rules, and removal procedures. The harness never grants
itself release, payment, or governance authority.

The proposed launch management rate is **$42 per hour for verified harness
manager work**, with a published monthly cap before any operative stage begins.
The rate and cap are design inputs, not a current wage or entitlement. Manager
costs would be accounted for with the other production costs before any future
surplus allocation. A later manager may take the role under the same published
rules or a separately approved revision; succession does not happen silently.

## 3. Project subscriber

A **project subscriber** is an adult who separately requests Crime Cartography
project emails through the project website. Project subscription is not a
YouTube subscription. It creates no entitlement to assignments, points,
payment, ownership, or selection for a later program.

The structured subscriber editorial workflow is not active during initial
launch. Hadi edits the introduction and first batch while public feedback
improves the harness. The workflow may activate after:

1. the project reaches 500 project subscribers; and
2. enough relevant feedback exists to design and test a responsible workflow.

The second condition still needs measurable acceptance criteria.

## 4. Editorial content unit

An **editorial content unit** is one bounded, pre-publication artifact sent with
one bounded question. Examples include:

- a beat or scene;
- a script or transcript excerpt;
- a chart or map;
- an audio segment;
- a visual treatment;
- a rough cut or full draft; or
- another participant's review with identifying details minimized.

Every task identifies the artifact revision, question, response deadline,
permitted use, confidentiality expectation, and evaluation rubric.

## 5. Editorial response

An **editorial response** identifies a concrete problem and proposes a useful
change. The first pilot may require at least 100 words, but word count is only
an empty-response filter. Length alone is not evidence of quality.

A stronger rubric should evaluate:

1. **specificity** — does the response name an exact claim, moment, or choice?
2. **support** — does it provide evidence, reasoning, or a concrete example?
3. **actionability** — can the production system test or apply the suggestion?
4. **impact** — would the change improve accuracy, clarity, pacing, safety, or
   memorability?
5. **originality** — does it add information beyond the task prompt and other
   responses?

## 6. Contribution record and experimental points

An accepted response may receive one **base contribution record**. Responses to
the same artifact revision may also be compared pairwise using the published
rubric. A response that is demonstrably more specific, supported, actionable,
and original may receive a **quality boost**.

Safeguards required before this affects any economic allocation:

- do not use word count as a quality score;
- do not reward agreement with the manager;
- compare only responses to the same content unit and question;
- disclose whether an LLM, a human, or both performed each evaluation;
- keep an auditable reason for every boost;
- test evaluator consistency and bias;
- detect copied, coordinated, padded, or prompt-injected responses;
- provide human review and an appeal path; and
- version the rubric so later rules do not silently rewrite earlier records.

Points are experimental project records. They are not money, stored value,
gift cards, wages, shares, or guaranteed future claims unless a later operative
agreement explicitly says otherwise after professional review.

## 7. Interaction surface

The first workflow uses a **website form backed by email**. During the initial
subscription pilot:

1. a visitor completes the Crime Cartography form on the Hadosh Academy site;
2. EmailJS sends a structured notification to
   `earthone+crimecarto@earthone.life`;
3. a separate read-only inbox worker validates and counts those requests
   directly from the EarthOne mailbox; and
4. no parallel subscriber database is created.

EmailJS and the email provider still process the submission. The form must say
that clearly. A request remains only a project-email request; it is not proof
of ownership of the address, eligibility, or consent to an economic program.
The machine-readable protocol and deployment gates are defined in
[`EMAIL-INTERFACE-CONTRACT.md`](EMAIL-INTERFACE-CONTRACT.md).

After the later community editorial workflow is activated:

1. the harness selects a bounded content unit;
2. the manager approves the task and recipient rules;
3. the harness emails the artifact pointer, question, rubric, and deadline;
4. the subscriber replies;
5. the harness records the response against the exact revision;
6. automated comparison may propose an evaluation;
7. a human resolves consequential or disputed judgments; and
8. the harness records what changed in the production artifact.

A later authenticated dashboard may replace or supplement email when identity,
privacy, access control, moderation, revision history, and appeals justify it.
The static public website remains the place to inspect and discuss the design;
it must not expose subscriber emails or private pre-publication media.

## 8. Feedback rights and privacy

Future operative terms must say what information is collected, how long it is
kept, who can see unpublished content, how a participant leaves, and what
license the participant grants the project to quote, summarize, adapt, and use
their feedback. Public comments and private editorial responses need separate
notices. Pre-publication access does not grant permission to redistribute an
artifact.

The v0.1 subscriber form records name, email, selected interests, consent-text
version, and request time. Its notification resides in the EarthOne mailbox;
the public repository stores the protocol but not submissions. Before outbound
subscriber mail begins, the system needs a confirmation step, a durable
suppression/unsubscribe method, a retention rule, and the disclosures required
for the actual class of messages being sent.

## 9. Matters intentionally unresolved

- measurable feedback-readiness criteria after 500 project subscribers;
- task frequency, selection, inactivity, and removal rules;
- evaluation sampling, tie handling, and appeal service levels;
- whether contribution records ever convert into an economic claim;
- worker, contractor, volunteer, contest, tax, privacy, and securities
  classification;
- eligibility and identity verification;
- reserve, cost, cap, overflow, accounting, and dispute rules; and
- the manager's hourly-rate evidence, monthly cap, approval, timesheet, and
  succession rules;
- management succession and collective governance.

Each term should be challenged independently. A public comment improves the
draft; it does not make a proposed term operative.
