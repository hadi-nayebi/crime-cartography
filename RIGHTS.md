# Rights and licensing boundary

This repository deliberately separates open production machinery from content,
data, brands, and third-party materials.

## Source code

Original source code and accompanying software documentation are licensed under
the repository's [MIT License](LICENSE).

## Rendered media and editorial content

The MIT License does **not** grant rights to rendered videos, narration,
scripts, music, thumbnails, channel artwork, logos, or other editorial and
brand assets unless a specific file states otherwise. Those works remain all
rights reserved by their respective rights holders.

Publishing source machinery, configuration, checksums, provenance, or release
metadata does not license the resulting audiovisual work. Finished Crime
Cartography media is distributed through the dedicated channel, not as an
open-licensed repository artifact.

The maps repository does not track finished thumbnails or channel-art binaries.
The public Academy website may serve the minimum profile/banner assets required
to render the project page and channel identity; public delivery does not place
those files under the MIT software license.

## Data and maps

Each dataset under `data/` retains the license and terms of its upstream source,
recorded in the dataset's `PROVENANCE.md`. Basemap and other third-party
attribution requirements remain in force. Crime data shows reported incidents,
often at block level rather than exact addresses; it is not a measure of
conviction or individual guilt.

## Third-party dependencies and assets

Dependencies, fonts, music, source data, basemaps, and other third-party
materials retain their own licenses. Their presence in a local production
workspace does not relicense them under MIT.
