# Participation, governance, and value

Part of the [Crime Cartography shared context](INDEX.md).

Status: **public working design**. Nothing in this file is an employment offer,
contest, ownership grant, compensation promise, or active redemption program.

## Three ways to participate `[consolidated]`

### 1. Public review

Anyone with a GitHub account can challenge a project definition, milestone,
editorial standard, technical design, or unresolved policy in its canonical
Discussion. Public comments are visible and may be summarized in later project
revisions.

### 2. Project emails

A person may separately request project updates through the Academy form.
Project subscription is not a YouTube subscription and creates no entitlement
to tasks, points, compensation, ownership, or selection.

The current form is an intake request only. Confirmation, unsubscribe,
suppression, retention, eligibility, and outbound-task operations must be tested
before the email editorial system is called active.

### 3. Future editorial cohort

After 500 project requests plus sufficient operational evidence, the project
may open a capped email cohort. A proposed first cap is 100 active reviewers,
while the general email list remains open. Both the activation criteria and cap
remain draft.

## Editorial content unit `[draft]`

One revision-bound pre-publication artifact sent with one bounded question.
Examples include a chart, map, transcript excerpt, audio segment, visual
treatment, rough cut, or another review with identifying details minimized.

Every unit must identify:

1. the exact artifact and revision;
2. the question and evaluation rubric; and
3. the deadline, permitted use, and confidentiality expectation.

## Editorial response `[draft]`

A response identifies a concrete problem and proposes a useful improvement.
A 100-word minimum may filter empty responses, but length is not quality.

The working rubric asks:

1. Is it specific and supported?
2. Is it actionable and likely to matter?
3. Does it add judgment beyond the prompt or other responses?

Agreement with the manager is not a scoring criterion.

## Contribution record `[draft]`

An accepted response may receive one base record. Responses to the same content
unit may be compared for a quality boost. Before this can affect any future
economic model, the system needs:

- a versioned rubric and auditable reason;
- disclosed human/LLM roles and bias testing; and
- human appeal and correction paths.

During an editorial beta, the recommended default is to preserve contribution
history with **no guaranteed economic conversion**. The project owner has not
yet consolidated that choice.

## Email-first operating model `[consolidated direction; future operation]`

1. The static website sends a structured EmailJS notification to
   `earthone+crimecarto@earthone.life`.
2. A private read-only mailbox worker validates requests and replies.
3. Subscriber addresses and private editorial material stay outside public Git.

No separate subscriber database is created by default. A private VPS may later
run the harness and mailbox worker when local operation is no longer enough.
Email remains the interface until identity, moderation, access control, or
revision needs prove that a portal is necessary.

The protocol specification is
[`experiment/EMAIL-INTERFACE-CONTRACT.md`](../experiment/EMAIL-INTERFACE-CONTRACT.md).

## Manager role and rate `[consolidated role; draft economics]`

Hadi is the launch harness manager and editor. The manager controls release,
handles consequential judgment, and remains accountable for the channel.

The proposed rate is **$42/hour for verified harness-manager work**, with a
published monthly cap before any compensation stage becomes operative. The
rate, evidence method, cap, approval process, and succession rules remain open.

## Value-distribution model `[draft]`

The project is exploring a future model in which:

1. verified production costs and capped manager costs are covered;
2. a minimum reserve protects operation; and
3. only the remaining distributable surplus is considered for allocation.

The working idea dedicates 90% of distributable surplus to qualifying
contributors and 10% to the manager, with individual caps and overflow returning
to the reserve. This is a design hypothesis, not an operative promise.

## Reserve or war chest `[draft]`

The reserve protects production continuity and may later support new formats,
new projects, or approved nonprofit contributions. Open questions include:

- its minimum and maximum;
- which costs it may cover;
- who approves overflow uses.

## Required gates before economic activation `[consolidated]`

No economic stage activates without all three:

1. **Operational evidence** — stable task, scoring, appeal, privacy, accounting,
   and release workflows.
2. **Qualified review** — legal, tax, worker-classification, privacy,
   securities/contest, and accounting review appropriate to the final model.
3. **Operative terms** — a versioned agreement, effective date, eligibility
   rules, participant consent, records, and a dispute path.

Public discussion can improve the design. It cannot substitute for these gates.

## Decisions still open
