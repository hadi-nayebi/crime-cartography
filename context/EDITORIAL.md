# Editorial system

Part of the [Crime Cartography shared context](INDEX.md).

## Viewer promise `[consolidated]`

After approximately five minutes, a viewer should retain three things:

1. **Time** — the defensible long arc and any important change in measurement.
2. **Place** — how the recent pattern is distributed at the source's honest
   geographic resolution.
3. **Meaning** — which categories and sourced context anchors make the pattern
   useful to remember.

Every video must also disclose material limits. Engagement should come from
understanding accumulating, not from sensational framing or an empty payoff.

## What the harness contributes `[consolidated]`

The harness can repeat work at scale:

- source discovery and provenance tracking;
- normalization, comparison, mapping, and chart assembly;
- draft copy, narration, visual sequencing, and consistency checks;
- revision routing, release checks, and production records.

The harness is good at producing a technically complete first pass. It cannot
certify that the result is worth a person's attention.

## What people contribute `[consolidated]`

People provide what the production system does not reliably have:

- contextual judgment and local knowledge;
- skepticism about valid-looking but misleading comparisons;
- taste in pacing, emphasis, language, sound, and visual hierarchy;
- moral judgment about privacy, stigma, and framing;
- the final decision about what must change before release.

This is the project's answer to the “AI slop” problem: high-volume output can
be technically complete while remaining generic, shallow, poorly framed, or
not worth remembering. The human layer must make revision-specific
improvements, not merely express approval or preference.

## A concrete before-and-after

The first-pass Boston chart can truthfully say:

> Reported index crime fell 71% from its 1989 peak to 2015.

That statement is accurate, but incomplete. A useful human editorial pass asks
three better questions:

1. **Where does the ruler change?** Mark that FBI annual index-crime counts and
   Boston Police incident records use different systems and should not be read
   as one seamless measure.
2. **What makes up the movement?** Show stable category composition where the
   data supports it instead of making the total line carry the whole story.
3. **What deserves a memory anchor?** Add only sourced local or national context
   that helps the viewer locate the chart in time, while avoiding unsupported
   causal claims.

The contribution is not “make the chart prettier.” It is a traceable correction
that makes the video both more engaging and more useful.

## Three-part video architecture `[draft]`

### 1. Read the long arc

Show the longest defensible citywide series, its peak or turning shape, and
every material measurement seam.

### 2. Open the city

Move from the total into category composition and recent spatial distribution.
Disclose coverage, missing coordinates, boundary changes, excluded records,
and source limitations where they affect interpretation.

### 3. Leave a durable memory

Close with no more than three verified takeaways and a small number of sourced
context anchors. A viewer should be able to explain the shape a week later
without remembering every number.

## Context anchor `[consolidated]`

A context anchor is a sourced event or transition that helps viewers locate a
chart in lived time.

It contains:

- a year or bounded date;
- a short viewer-facing label;
- a reliable source;
- why it helps orientation; and
- whether it affects the data measure or is context only.

A nearby event is not automatically a cause. Causal language requires causal
evidence.

## Release test `[consolidated]`

A dedicated remake is not releasable until a human can answer yes to all three:

1. **Is it true?** Claims recompute from the release data and material limits
   are visible.
2. **Is it useful?** The viewer receives a coherent time-place-meaning model.
3. **Is it responsible?** The framing minimizes victim exposure, neighborhood
   stigma, false precision, and sensational implication.

A changed render invalidates approval tied to older bytes.

## Current production warnings

- Chicago maps 88.6% of records to its spatial units.
- Kansas City maps 86.8%.
- Seattle maps 51.3% and needs a materially different disclosure or treatment.
- Grand Rapids has aggregate geography only because its source does not provide
  point coordinates.

These are release constraints, not footnotes to hide.
