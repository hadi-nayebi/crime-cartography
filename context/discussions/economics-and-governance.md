# Design value distribution, reserve, and governance

Crime Cartography is exploring whether a human-managed production harness can
share created value with people who perform useful editorial work. No economic
program is active.

## What is settled

- YouTube engagement is not paid activity.
- Project subscription creates no right to payment, ownership, or work.
- Verified costs, a capped manager cost, and a reserve come before any future
  surplus allocation.
- Operative economics require professional review, versioned terms, an effective
  date, and explicit consent.

## Current working model

- proposed manager rate: `$42/hour`, monthly cap unresolved;
- proposed split of distributable surplus: 90% qualifying contributors / 10%
  manager;
- individual caps and reserve overflow remain undefined.

These are design hypotheses, not promises.

## Three questions

1. How should verified costs, manager time, and the reserve be defined and
   audited?
2. Should beta contribution history have no guaranteed conversion, or should
   tasks wait until contingent terms exist?
3. Who should approve reserve overflow, manager succession, and later changes
   to the allocation model?
